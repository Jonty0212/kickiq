This Privacy Notice explains how KickIQ collects, uses, and protects your personal data in accordance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1 Who We Are

KickIQ is a rugby kicking coaching community founded and owned by Dylan Kay. The WhatsApp community is managed by Jonty Culliford, who acts as the data controller responsible for how your personal data is collected and used.

Data controller: Jonty Culliford (KickIQ Community Manager)
Contact: kickiqrugby@gmail.com

2 What Data We Collect

2.1 Data you provide directly

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Instagram handle or phone number (provided at early access sign-up)
  • Payment information (processed securely by Stripe — we do not store card details)

2.2 Data collected automatically

  • Date and time of sign-up
  • Membership tier and payment history

2.3 Data from WhatsApp

When you join the KickIQ WhatsApp Community, Meta (WhatsApp's parent company) may process your data according to their own privacy policy. We recommend reviewing WhatsApp's privacy policy at whatsapp.com/legal/privacy-policy.

3 How We Use Your Data

  • To process your membership and provide access to the community
  • To contact you about your membership, including renewals and important updates
  • To send announcements and community communications via WhatsApp
  • To respond to your queries and support requests
  • To improve our service and community offering

We will not use your data for any purpose other than those listed above without your explicit consent.

4 Legal Basis for Processing

  • Contract — processing is necessary to provide the membership service you have subscribed to
  • Legitimate interests — to manage and improve our community, and to communicate with members about relevant updates
  • Consent — where you have given us specific consent (for example, to be contacted about early access)

5 Who We Share Data With

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes. We may share data with the following trusted service providers:

  • Stripe — payment processing
  • Meta (WhatsApp) — community communication platform
  • Google — sign-up data storage (Google Sheets)

These providers are only permitted to use your data to provide their services to us. We may disclose your data if required to do so by law or by a regulatory authority.

6 How Long We Keep Your Data

  • Membership and payment records: 7 years (required for tax purposes)
  • Email and contact data: deleted within 12 months of membership ending, unless you request earlier deletion
  • Early access enquiry data: deleted within 6 months if membership is not taken up

7 Your Rights

Under UK GDPR you have the following rights:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — ask us to delete your data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Right to restrict processing — ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a structured, commonly used format
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at kickiqrugby@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days at no charge.

8 Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorised access, loss, or misuse. Payment data is handled entirely by Stripe and is never stored on our systems.

9 Cookies & Tracking

Our sign-up page does not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking. No personal data is collected through cookies. If this changes we will update this notice and inform members.

10 Changes to This Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time. Where changes are significant, we will notify members via the KickIQ Announcements channel. The date at the top of this document reflects when it was last updated.

11 How to Complain

If you are unhappy with how we handle your personal data, please contact us first at kickiqrugby@gmail.com.

If you remain unsatisfied, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office:

Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
Website: ico.org.uk
